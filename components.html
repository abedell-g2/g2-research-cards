Foundation

5 Query Shapes for Software Research

Based on how buyers research business software on review sites. Each shape represents a distinct intent pattern — the signal in the query determines what a genuinely useful response looks like.

1

Category Discovery

Pain-first

Signal: "I need something to help me with X" — user knows the pain, not the category

  • "I need a better way to manage business expenses"
  • "Software to help our team communicate better"
  • "Something to track our sales pipeline"
2

Product Info

Named product

Signal: "Is [product] good?" — highest confidence, user already has a product in mind

  • "Is Salesforce worth it for a small business?"
  • "How good is HubSpot for marketing?"
  • "What do people think of Monday.com?"
3

Comparison

Evaluating

Signal: "[product A] vs [product B]" — high purchase intent, narrowing down a shortlist

  • "Salesforce vs HubSpot for mid-market"
  • "Asana vs Monday.com for project management"
  • "Zendesk vs Intercom for customer support"
4

Alternatives

Switching intent

Signal: "Alternatives to [product]" — already using something, actively looking to leave

  • "Alternatives to Salesforce that are easier to use"
  • "Cheaper alternatives to Zendesk"
  • "Something like Notion but with better task management"
5

Best-in-Category

Open exploration

Signal: "Best [category] software" — knows the category, wants a trusted starting point

  • "Best CRM software for small business"
  • "Top project management tools for remote teams"
  • "Best help desk software with a free tier"
