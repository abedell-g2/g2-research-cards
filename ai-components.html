Foundation

10 Query Shapes for AI Software

Based on research into how buyers research AI tools on review sites. Three shapes are completely unique to AI — they have no analog in general software buying. Two more have AI-specific nuances around pricing and accuracy that change what a useful answer looks like.

1

Capability Discovery

AI-unique

Signal: "Can AI do X?" — user doesn't know if AI can help at all

  • "Can AI write my marketing emails for me?"
  • "Is there AI that can do code review?"
  • "Can AI analyze my spreadsheet data?"
2

AI Product Evaluation

Standard

Signal: "Is [AI tool] good?" — but with trust layer: accuracy, data policy

  • "Is GitHub Copilot actually worth it?"
  • "Is Jasper AI any good for marketing?"
  • "Is Otter.ai accurate enough for business?"
3

AI Comparison

AI-nuanced

Signal: "[AI A] vs [AI B]" — new dimensions: model quality, context window, accuracy

  • "ChatGPT vs Claude for writing"
  • "GitHub Copilot vs Cursor"
  • "Jasper vs Copy.ai"
4

AI Alternatives

Standard

Signal: "Alternatives to [AI tool]" — often escaping cost, accuracy, or privacy

  • "Alternatives to ChatGPT for business"
  • "Free alternatives to Jasper AI"
  • "Open source alternatives to GitHub Copilot"
5

Task-Based Rankings

AI-nuanced

Signal: "Best AI for [task]" — category is the task, not a software type

  • "Best AI writing tool for SEO"
  • "Best AI coding assistant for Python"
  • "Best AI for meeting transcription"

Trust & Privacy

AI-unique

Signal: "Is [AI tool] safe?" — trains on data? GDPR? HIPAA?

  • "Does ChatGPT store my company data?"
  • "Which AI tools are HIPAA compliant?"
  • "Does GitHub Copilot train on my code?"
7

Build vs. Buy

Accuracy & Reliability
